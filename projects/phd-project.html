Physics-Aware Learning from High-Order CFD

Ongoing research · Preprint in preparation · Code not publicly available

Overview

This research develops a physics-aware machine learning framework for accelerating computational fluid dynamics (CFD) simulations that use high-order numerical methods. Traditional neural surrogate models often treat numerical solutions as generic field data, discarding the structured information embedded in the discretization scheme. Our approach preserves the discretization structure—solution points, local numerical coupling, and boundary adjacency—as learning primitives within a graph-based model. By encoding the geometry, boundary conditions, and solver topology directly into the learning architecture, the resulting surrogate maintains numerical consistency and produces stable, boundary-aware predictions suitable for autoregressive rollouts in complex flow scenarios.
